About Real Estate

Have a question? Please check our knowledgebase first.
What are the implications of entering into a lease agreement?

By signing a lease agreement, a tenant agrees to pay a specific amount of rent for a specific period of time. This means that the tenant is obligated to pay rent on time and in full for the entire duration of the lease, even if they no longer want to live in the property. The landlord, on the other hand, is obligated to provide a habitable dwelling and maintain the property in good condition.

How do I know If I am ready to buy a home ?

Stable income: You should have a stable source of income that allows you to comfortably afford monthly mortgage payments, property taxes, and other associated costs.

How does purchasing a home compare with renting?

Renting and owning a home both have pros and cons. Renting typically provides more flexibility and fewer responsibilities, while owning a home can provide long-term financial benefits and a sense of stability. Homeownership also comes with additional expenses, such as property taxes and maintenance costs. Ultimately, the decision to rent or buy should be based on your individual circumstances and financial goals.

What should I look for when walking through a home ?

The overall condition of the home, including any signs of damage or wear and tear.The layout and flow of the space, including the size of rooms and how they connect to each other.

How do I figure out what mortgage I can afford ?

To figure out what mortgage you can afford, you should consider your income, expenses, and debt. Generally, your monthly mortgage payment should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income. You should also factor in other expenses such as property taxes, insurance, and maintenance costs. It's important to consult with a mortgage professional to get a better understanding of what you can afford.

How many homes should I consider before choosing one ?

There's no set number of homes you should consider before making a decision. It's important to take the time to thoroughly research and view properties until you find the one that meets your needs and budget. Keep in mind that the home-buying process can take time, so it's important to be patient and not rush into a decision.
